Regions

COLUMBIA

Cocoa from the lowlands of the Santander region is rich in nutty flavor. It’s naturally sweet, with notes of cherry, bitter almond and even a hint of toasted marshmallow off a campfire. Gail uses Colombian chocolate to make her bars and envelop all of her truffles and tumbled treats in a silky smoothness that still has a firm snap when you take a bite.

COSTA RICA

In the rainy, languid lowlands of north-central Costa Rica, this traditional crop was nearly wiped out by disease three decades ago. This is big-flavored, truly dark chocolate with waves of black olive and toast that flood your palate

ECUADOR

Ecuador’s lowlands produce some of the most flavorful cacao in the world.Grown only in Ecuador, Arriba is a rare confluence of floral notes jasmine and orange blossom that finishes with a smooth, smokey earthiness. This chocolate is certified by the Rainforest Alliance.

PERU

The jungles of Peru have long been a source of high quality chocolate. A 65% Peruvian dark chocolate that’s simply magical — tasting of sharp, fresh blueberries and the pure, earthy depths of cacao.
